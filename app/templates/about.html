About oddsinshadow

Understanding probabilistic forecasts for major football leagues

What We Do (in the shadows)

oddsinshadow provides probabilistic forecasts for major football leagues around the world. Currently we provide forecatss for the Big 5 European club Leagues and the three UEFA club tournaments. Using statistical models and Elo rating systems, we calculate the likelihood of various outcomes for teams throughout the season.

Our forecasts help fans, analysts, and enthusiasts understand not just who might win, but the probability of different scenarios playing out across domestic leagues and continental competitions.

How It Works

For the models we currently have up-and-running, we use elo ratings to determine the team's strengths, which we get directly from the always reliable website clubelo.com. We could potentially have our own calculation of the team's strengths, but clubelo is already doing a great job at this, and they have a functioning API that lets us get ELOs on a day-to-day basis.

With these elo ratings we can derive how many goals each team will score on average, and then using a Poisson model we can actually simulate what the final score of the game will be. We do this about 10,000 times for every scheduled game, and voila we have an estimate of how frequently each team wins the league. Nerds tend to call this a Monte Carlo simulation.

After we run these 10,000 simulations we can then provide estimates of each team's likelihood to win the title, as well as other key races such as landing a top-4 spot for UCL qualification, and relegation battles.
